Validated Patient-Centred Communication Framework for Pharmacists · Malaysia

From Directive Monologue to Collaborative Dialogue

CONNECT-6 is a structured, evidence-based medication counselling framework that operationalises Motivational Interviewing into a practical 6-step workflow — designed for pharmacists working in time-constrained clinical settings.

Understanding CONNECT-6

A short explainer on what CONNECT-6 is, why it was developed, and how it works in practice.

The Gap

Why CONNECT-6 Was Needed

Despite strong international frameworks, pharmacists consistently struggle to deliver patient-centred counselling in real-world clinical settings.

Before CONNECT-6

Medication counselling was often directive and pharmacist-centric — "one-way communication" resembling parent-to-child instructions. Patients felt like passive receivers. Information was overwhelming. The gap between behavioural theory and daily practice remained wide, particularly in multilingual, time-constrained public-sector settings.

What CONNECT-6 Does

CONNECT-6 translates Motivational Interviewing and complementary behavioural models — including the Health Action Process Approach and Common Sense Model — into a structured, learnable 6-step consultation workflow. It was co-designed for Malaysian public-sector pharmacy environments and validated across hospital and health clinic settings.

The Framework

Six Steps. One Conversation.

Each step maps to established behavioural theory while remaining flexible enough to adapt to real patient needs. Steps CHECK, CLARIFY, and COACH can be revisited or reordered based on patient cues.

1
CONNECT

Build Trust Through Genuine Connection

Create a safe psychological environment where the patient feels heard and respected — reducing defensiveness before clinical issues are addressed.

2
CLARIFY

Uncover Barriers to Adherence

Diagnose whether non-adherence stems from beliefs, logistics, or knowledge gaps. Explore using the Belief Explorer and WHO 5 Dimensions of Adherence Barriers.

3
COACH

Share Knowledge Collaboratively

Educate based on the patient's identified needs using the Elicit–Provide–Elicit technique. Ask permission before providing targeted information.

4
CHECK

Gauge Readiness for Change

Assess the patient's motivation (importance) and self-efficacy (confidence) using the Readiness Ruler and Confidence Check tools.

5
CREATE

Build the Solution Together

Co-create a specific, feasible plan the patient owns — not a directive instruction. Offer options, invite input, and strengthen commitment collaboratively.

6
COMMIT

Lock the Plan & Follow Up

Turn the plan into a concrete commitment. Summarise using SMART goals, elicit verbal commitment, reinforce self-efficacy, and arrange follow-up support.

The Evidence

Validated. Significant. Scalable.

A mixed-methods pre–post interventional study was conducted with 27 pharmacists across hospital and health clinic settings in Selangor, Malaysia.

AI-Assisted Self-Learning Platform

CONNECT-6 was built on the belief that every pharmacist — regardless of location or resources — should be able to deliver patient-centred counselling. To extend that mission further, a dedicated digital platform is in development that will allow pharmacists to train on CONNECT-6 independently, using AI-powered roleplay simulation, interactive scenario practice, and competency feedback — without needing a live facilitator.

The Team

About the CONNECT-6 Research Team

CONNECT-6 was developed and validated by a multidisciplinary team spanning clinical pharmacy, pharmacy education, and health psychology — across hospital, academic, and specialist settings in Malaysia.

Fahmi Hassan, PhD

Lead Developer & Principal Contact · Pharmacist, HTAR Klang

Dr Fahmi Hassan is a pharmacist and clinical pharmacy researcher at Hospital Tengku Ampuan Rahimah (HTAR), Klang. His PhD focuses on social and administrative pharmacy, with a sustained interest in medication counselling and healthcare service delivery research. He led the development and validation of the CONNECT-6 framework, bringing together a multidisciplinary team spanning clinical pharmacy, pharmacy education, and health psychology.

For training enquiries, research collaborations, and institutional partnerships, the team welcomes contact from interested individuals and institutions.
